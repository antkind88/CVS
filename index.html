Caleb Kaufman

Bio

Caleb Kaufman graduated with a degree in Biopsychology from New College of Florida in 2010. His focus was on neuroscience and philosophy.

Caleb learned HTML4 and CSS at the age of 17, and began designing custom fundraising websites for non-profits using a custom ASP.net templating engine.

(Caleb does not miss designing with tables).

Through working at his father's software company, Synergy Development Systems Inc., Caleb has gotten exposure to both the business end of SAAS, and the development side. He has worked in technical support and training roles, and has helped supervise custom API contracts for various NGOs, having to carefully document their requirements and feedback on their website design.

Caleb assisted in a total redesign of Synergy Inc.'s flagship product, DenariOnline. Through collating data from customer surveys, Caleb collaborated with the lead developer to implement a more logical navigation and workflow, as well as the implementation of most requested features (Denari 2).

Caleb also designed a "light" version of the main flagship product for startup NGOs, which included a custom drag-and-drop donation page editor (eDenari).

After receiving training in SCRUM, Caleb collaborated with a six person team to develop a crowdfunding website with an alternative payment model.

At Phase Technologies, Caleb had the oppourtunity to dig in to javascript, working as a junior developer on their closed-domain custom manufactoring software. He specifically developed new features for the sales CRM, and collaborated with various departments at Phase to find and fix bugs, and develop new features.

Caleb has continued contract web design for small businesses and friends, and has worked Advantage Marketing as a Wordpress Developer. He has several personal projects, and strives to continue to learn both the hard and soft skills required for software development.
